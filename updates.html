Global Visa Policy & Updates

Stay informed with the latest immigration news and policy changes.

Canada Nov 19, 2025

Fast Tracks Top Talent: Master's and PhD Students Exempt from National Cap

In a strategic policy shift announced by IRCC, Canada is prioritizing high-skilled international students. The most critical change is the exemption of Master's and PhD students from the Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL) requirement.

Key Benefits for Our Clients:

  • Guaranteed Spot: Your application is removed from the high-risk pool of provincial caps.
  • Fast Track: PhD students benefit from an accelerated processing target of just 14 days.

Official guidance published by IRCC.

UK Nov 14, 2025

Students Can Now Switch to Innovator Founder Visa In-Country

Effective November 25, 2025, Student visa holders who have completed their course can apply to switch directly to the Innovator Founder visa from within the UK. This eliminates the need to return home to apply.

Client Benefit:

  • Business Continuity: Transition research into a commercial reality without leaving the UK.
  • Settlement Path: Leads to Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) in as little as three years.
Australia Nov 14, 2025

Ministerial Direction 115 (MD115) Takes Effect Today

Replaces MD111. Links visa processing priority directly to an education provider’s compliance with government planning levels. Tier 1 Providers get the fastest processing.

Action Required:

Choosing a Tier 1 (Priority) education provider is now the most effective way to secure a rapid visa outcome. Consult with us to ensure your provider is ranked highly.

UK Nov 11, 2025

Stricter Character Rules Apply to Family and Private Life Visas

The Home Office has introduced 'Part Suitability', a consolidated set of rules tightening requirements for good character. Past breaches (overstaying, deception) are now centrally located grounds for refusal.

Warning: Clients with any history of immigration breaches must be fully transparent with SNS Visa Consultants.

UK Nov 11, 2025

Student Visa Maintenance Funds Increase Takes Effect

The minimum monthly maintenance funds required have increased to align with the cost of living:

  • London: £1,529 per month.
  • Outside London: £1,171 per month.

Funds must be held for a minimum of 28 consecutive days.

USA Nov 5, 2025

DV Lottery Registration for DV-2027 Officially Delayed

The registration start date has been postponed to implement system upgrades and the new $1 electronic entry fee. Status is currently closed.

Warning: Avoid scams advertising fake registration dates. Wait for the official DOS announcement.

Canada Nov 4, 2025

New Rules Allow Visa Cancellation for Non-Compliance

Officers now have explicit authority to cancel existing permits if the holder fails to maintain compliance (e.g., stops attending classes, works unauthorized hours). This shifts focus from initial approval to ongoing adherence.

Canada Nov 4, 2025

Record Rejection Rate Signals Mandatory Shift in Application Quality

Refusal rates have surged as IRCC combats fraud and manages capacity. Scrutiny on "Genuine Student" and "Genuine Temporary Resident" intent is at an all-time high.

Strategy: Flawless submissions are necessary. Every document must be verifiable, and Study Plans must be logically watertight.

UK Nov 4, 2025

High Potential Individual (HPI) Visa Expansion

Eligibility expanded to graduates from any university ranked in the global top 100. An annual global cap of 8,000 applications has been introduced.

Benefit: Allows graduates to work in the UK for 2 years (3 for PhD) without employer sponsorship.

New Zealand Nov 3, 2025

Student Work Rights Increase to 25 Hours Per Week

Eligible tertiary students can now work up to 25 hours per week during term time (up from 20). Full-time work during scheduled breaks remains permitted.

Schengen Oct 12, 2025

EU Smart Borders (EES) System Goes Live

The automated IT system replacing manual stamping has launched. It digitally records entry/exit data (biometrics) to track the 90/180-day rule automatically. Expect slight delays during the initial registration phase on first entry.

UAE Oct 2025

Extended Stay Options: 30, 60, and 90 Day Visas

The UAE has standardized single-entry visas into 30, 60, and 90-day tiers. The 90-day option is typically reserved for visiting friends/relatives or business purposes, reducing the need for costly in-country extensions.

Australia Oct 5, 2025

Visa Speed Advantage with Ministerial Direction 111 (MD111)

MD111 prioritizes applications based on the institution's risk rating. Choosing a Priority 1 institution places your application in the fast lane for quicker decision-making.

Australia Oct 2, 2025

Sri Lanka Upgraded to Level 1 (Low Risk) for Student Visas

Sri Lanka has been upgraded to Evidence Level 1. This means streamlined documentation (fewer upfront financial/English checks) but requires strictly passing the new Genuine Student (GS) test.

New Zealand Sep 29, 2025

Parent Boost Visitor Visa Opens

Parents of NZ citizens/residents can now apply for a visitor visa allowing a stay of up to 5 years (renewable to 10). Requires sponsorship and mandatory health insurance.

Canada Sep 2025

Expansion of Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) Eligibility

IRCC continues to expand eligible programs for PGWP, focusing on high-demand sectors like STEM, Healthcare, and Skilled Trades. Choosing an aligned program maximizes career prospects.

Germany Sep 2025

VFS Global Now Handles Visa Applications in Sri Lanka

German visa processing has transitioned to VFS Global Colombo. All biometric data and documents must now be submitted at the centralized VAC, streamlining appointments and tracking.

USA Sep 16, 2025

New $1 Electronic Fee Mandated for DV Lottery Entry

A mandatory, non-refundable $1 USD fee applies to all DV-2027 entries to combat fraud and fund security upgrades. A valid electronic payment method is now required to enter.

Canada Sep 1, 2025

Crucial Increase in Student Visa Financial Requirements

Minimum funds required for living expenses (excluding tuition) have increased to CA$22,895 for a single applicant. Budgets must be adjusted immediately for new applications.
